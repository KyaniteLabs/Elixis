The Problem
WHO YOU ARE ISN'T
IN THE PROMPT.
01

Agents have no identity.

You can describe what an agent does. You can't describe who it is. AGENTS.md handles operations. Nothing handles identity.

02

Personality is an afterthought.

Most agent personality is retrofitted via system prompts. That's not identity — that's costuming.

03

The pattern is invisible.

Tony Montana AND Hannah Montana AND The Godfather? There's a reason. But no tool finds it.

04

It's not transferable.

A good identity file works across frameworks. OpenClaw, Aeon, Nanobot, ZeroClaw — all read SOUL.md.

The Process
THE GLASS BEAD
GAME.
Stage 01
Extract Entities
Parse your brain dump into typed references — characters, films, books, historical figures. One per line, freeform, doesn't matter.
Stage 02
Pattern Graph Engine
Every entity votes on every pattern. Probabilities emerge — not a single answer, a full probability space with bridges connecting patterns. Consensus score tells you how defined the identity is.
Stage 03
SOUL.md Synthesis
OpenClaw-spec identity document. Worldview, voice, principles, boundaries. Nothing that isn't identity. Readable by any framework that reads markdown.
The Output
TWO THINGS.
ONE PROCESS.
01

Analysis Dashboard

Visual, beautiful, explorable. Pattern probability graph, entity contribution scores, bridge concepts, consensus confidence. This is the art — it's not the output file.

02

SOUL.md

The actual identity document. Clean, minimal, OpenClaw-spec. Worldview, voice, principles, boundaries, pet peeves. Works in OpenClaw, Aeon, Nanobot, ZeroClaw, any framework.
